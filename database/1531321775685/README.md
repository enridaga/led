<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0cm 0cm 0.0001pt; font-size: medium; font-family: 'Times New Roman';"><span style="font-size: 11pt;">[Edna and John Bold lived for short intervals on each of the Aran Islands, County Gallway, Ireland, over a number of years]&nbsp;</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0cm 0cm 0.0001pt; font-size: medium; font-family: 'Times New Roman';"><span style="font-size: 11pt;">&nbsp;</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0cm 0cm 0.0001pt; font-size: medium; font-family: 'Times New Roman';"><span style="font-size: 11pt;">It was early evening when we collected Colman Cohnelly and Otto Shaw from Pat Hernon&rsquo;s bar on Inishmaan. The two men and their respective crews had whiled away the time talking and drinking.</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0cm 0cm 0.0001pt; font-size: medium; font-family: 'Times New Roman';"><span style="font-size: 11pt;">[&hellip;]</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0cm 0cm 0.0001pt; font-size: medium; font-family: 'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;</p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0cm 0cm 0.0001pt; font-size: medium; font-family: 'Times New Roman';"><span style="font-size: 11pt;">There was little to indicate that they were drunk as we descended the bleak road to the shore. [...]</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0cm 0cm 0.0001pt; font-size: medium; font-family: 'Times New Roman';"><span style="font-size: 11pt;">&nbsp;</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0cm 0cm 0.0001pt; font-size: medium; font-family: 'Times New Roman';"><span style="font-size: 11pt;">All this time [as the crew prepared two boats] the befuddled men had spoken little. Now they began to sing. Gaelic singing can be spine chilling and wild. The &lsquo;Fiery Jack&rsquo; in their veins had not yet cooled.&nbsp;</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0cm 0cm 0.0001pt; font-size: medium; font-family: 'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;</p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0cm 0cm 0.0001pt; font-size: medium; font-family: 'Times New Roman';">The sky was darkening.&nbsp; Clouds raced and hurled their white, grey, black plumes of wavering, quavering vapour high into the heavens.&nbsp; Thunder boomed. The sea heaved in a swell black as treacle.&nbsp; Inishere looked like a thin, bleached bone in the distance.&nbsp; It would take an hour to cross the sound.</p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0cm 0cm 0.0001pt; font-size: medium; font-family: 'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;</p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0cm 0cm 0.0001pt; font-size: medium; font-family: 'Times New Roman';"><span style="font-size: 11pt;">We lay back in the curragh, relaxed, exhilarated, quickened by the light of pure unreason. When we should have been most afraid we were not afraid. We had become part of the sky, the sea, the tossing boat and the strange, eerie song of the men in whose expertise we had complete trust.&nbsp;</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0cm 0cm 0.0001pt; font-size: medium; font-family: 'Times New Roman';"><span style="font-size: 11pt;">[&hellip;]</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0cm 0cm 0.0001pt; font-size: medium; font-family: 'Times New Roman';"><span style="font-size: 11pt;">&nbsp;</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0cm 0cm 0.0001pt; font-size: medium; font-family: 'Times New Roman';"><span style="font-size: 11pt;">Translated by Colman&rsquo;s sister, Mary, the spine chilling song of the oarsmen as they crossed the treacly water of the Sound, turned out to be more in the genre of an English ballad than a ferocious celtic saga of &lsquo;daring do&rsquo;. How the wild singing came to be &lsquo;married&rsquo; to a narrative of an affair of the heart is not to be explained nor conveyed here.&nbsp;</span></p>

The recollections of Edna Bold, born in Manchester (1904) are dated 1978. They are arranged thematically and were written for her grandnieces and nephews. The memoir is available at the Burnett Archive of Working Class Autobiographies, gathered together by John Burnett, David Vincent and David Mayall whilst compiling their three volumes annotated bibliography, "The Autobiography of the Working Class" (Harvester Press, 1984-1989)
