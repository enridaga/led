<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-pagination: none; mso-layout-grid-align: none; text-autospace: none;"><span lang="EN-US" style="font-family: Arial; mso-bidi-font-family: Arial; mso-ansi-language: EN-US;">The Living in <em>Venice</em> is like being on board a vast Ship; out of which you go now and then for Airing in the Long-boat. All their Diversions of taking the Air are upon the Water (where else indeed must they have them?) there they take the <em>Fresco,</em> as they call it, (for 'tisin the Cool of the Evening,) where the <em>Gondola's</em> wheel about, pass and re-pass on the great Canal, just as the Coaches do in <em>Hyde</em>-Park. This they do every Holiday Evening, of which they have there in good store.[&hellip;] </span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-pagination: none; mso-layout-grid-align: none; text-autospace: none;"><span style="font-family: Arial;">We were at a Diversion of this sort one day upon the </span><em style="font-family: Arial;">Lagune,</em><span style="font-family: Arial;"> near the Church of </span><em style="font-family: Arial;">la Gratia,</em><span style="font-family: Arial;"> occasion'd by a Benediction that was there of a Ship-load of Pilgrims, who were setting out upon their Holy Voyage. They have sometimes Serenades upon the Water, of instrumental and vocal Musick, Song and </span><em style="font-family: Arial;">Recitativo,</em><span style="font-family: Arial;"> after the Manner of the Opera's.</span></p>
