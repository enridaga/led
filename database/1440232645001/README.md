## excerpt from 'Sergey Prokofiev diaries: 11 November 1914' pp. 775 (143 words)

### language


*urn:iso:std:iso:639:ed-3:eng*

### value


<p>At three o'clock I went to Koussevitzky's rehearsal to hear Myaskovsky's&nbsp;<em>Alastor</em>. Because the Court Hall has been given over to house the wounded, Koussevitzky has to prevent his concerts in the Maly Theatre*, with the orchestra on the stage where the sound is very dull.&nbsp;<em>Forte&nbsp;</em>passages simply disappear; when the flutes play&nbsp;<em>piano&nbsp;</em>or the double-basses&nbsp;<em>pianissimo</em>, they can be heard only by straining the ears. In&nbsp;<em>Alastor</em>, at the best of times a piece with its longueurs, Koussevitzky dragged out the tempi, thereby doing serious damage to the work. Much of it afforded me great pleasure, however, even though as a whole the work is, if I may put it so, lacking in compactness. But its sound-world is wonderful, in places truly remarkable. At the moment I am preoccupied with orchestral timbres and therefore place particular value on this aspect.</p>
