### comment


Henry Croswell (1840–93) kept a record of his visits to churches in London over a period of more than twelve years (1872–85).  He made methodical notes about the number of clergy, the churchmanship, the congregation, the sermon and the church architecture, as well as commenting on the music that he heard (the organ, the hymns and the choir).  The above listening experience has been extracted from one of these records.

<p class="MsoNormal">O[rgan]. &ndash;&nbsp;Large and powerful.</p>
<p class="MsoNormal">H[ymns]. &ndash;&nbsp;"Jesus, my happy home &hellip;" and a fine Anthem.&nbsp; It was Anglican music.</p>
<p class="MsoNormal">C[hoir]. &ndash;&nbsp;Large, surpliced, good singers.</p>
<p class="MsoNormal">[The congregation numbered] 1500 &ndash;&nbsp;Crowded.&nbsp; There were no vacant sittings.&nbsp; Extra seats were occupied in the aisles.&nbsp;[&hellip;]</p>
<p class="MsoNormal">S[ermon]. &ndash;&nbsp;We didn't stop.</p>
<p class="MsoNormal">[&hellip;]</p>
<p class="MsoNormal">M[iscellaneous]. &ndash;&nbsp;A fashionable church and so crowded.&nbsp; What a curious thing fashion is!&nbsp; Why should this be crowded and other equally well conducted churches be nearly empty.&nbsp; Is the neighbourhood and a swell vicar the reason?</p>
