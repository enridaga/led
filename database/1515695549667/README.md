<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-pagination: none; mso-layout-grid-align: none; text-autospace: none;"><span lang="EN-US" style="font-family: Arial; mso-bidi-font-family: Arial; mso-ansi-language: EN-US;">The Nuns of S. <em>Lorenzo,</em> and those of S. <em>Maria Celestia,</em> have on their Feast-days, one the 10th, the other the 15th of <em>August,</em> a great Concert of</span> <span lang="EN-US">Musick</span><span lang="EN-US" style="font-family: Arial; mso-bidi-font-family: Arial; mso-ansi-language: EN-US;"> in their several Churches. The Nuns of both these Convents are noble Ladies; and they vie for Superiority with each other, which shall have the best Musick: and therefore each obliges the chief of their </span><span lang="EN-US">Musicians</span><span lang="EN-US" style="font-family: Arial; mso-bidi-font-family: Arial; mso-ansi-language: EN-US;"> when they engage them to be at their Feast, not to be employed at the other. At the <em>Celestia</em> there was an occasional <em>Portico,</em> and a Colonnade on the Bridge that leads to the Church, with Extempore-Statues, made up of Pasteboard and stiffen'd Linen Cloth; both without the Church and within. The Churches on these Occasions are adorn'd with the richest Hangings they can get. Without doors these Viragoes have Guns firing, with Trumpets and Hautboys sounding, to make all the Noise they can.</span></p>
