<p class="MsoNormal">I have seen a Play, &amp; Opera acted by Fryars;<span style="mso-bidi-font-family: Calibri; mso-bidi-theme-font: minor-latin;">―</span>the Monks of S<sup>t</sup> Victor: those who were not employed on the Stage play&rsquo;d in the Orchestra, &amp; those who did neither<span style="mso-bidi-font-family: Calibri; mso-bidi-theme-font: minor-latin;">―</span>entertain&rsquo;d the Company:<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>[&hellip;] as no Women must be admitted to act with them on the Stage, they get Soprano Singers to represent the female Parts, and I saw <em style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;">them</em> blush at the Freedoms used to them by the Fryars in tender Scenes.&nbsp;</p>
<p class="MsoNormal">I must acknowledge that the Tragic part was very touching, &amp; affected me even to Tears: I was surprized likewise to see how well they understood those feelings of parental Fondness, from the Experience of which they were so completely excluded.</p>
