<p class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: justify; line-height: 12.55pt; mso-line-height-rule: exactly; margin: 0cm 56.0pt .0001pt 0cm;">The next piece was from an anthem by Pergolesi, &ldquo;O Lord, have mercy upon me,&rdquo; a most beautiful solo of two movements; the first highly pathetic; the second, &ldquo;But my hope hath been in thee,&rdquo; cheerful and exhilarating. It was sung by Phillips with feeling and expression; but I did not think it well suited to his voice, as I had heard it sung by a contralto, which appears more appropriate for it than a bass. Yet no fault was to be found with his performance.</p>
