<p class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: justify; line-height: 12.55pt; mso-line-height-rule: exactly; margin: 0cm 56.0pt .0001pt 0cm;">Then followed the beginning of a &ldquo;Gloria in excelsis,&rdquo; by Pergolesi, in which two boys were introduced, choristers of the Chapel Royal and Abbey. They had sweet voices, but it was very injudicious to employ them, as there was no <em style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;">want</em> certainly of sopranos. Boys can never sing really well; and though their clear <em style="mso-bidi-font-style: normal;">bell-like</em> tones have often a charming effect in the cathedral services, they are not sufficiently formed to be brought forward as principals on an occasion like this. Such exhibitions are like schoolboys reciting their lessons in public, a thing not to be done except before select and partial audiences.</p>
