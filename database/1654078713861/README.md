<p class="MsoNormal"><span style="mso-ascii-font-family: Calibri; mso-fareast-font-family: Calibri; mso-hansi-font-family: Calibri; mso-bidi-font-family: 'Times New Roman';">Localized or Yorkshire sentimental songs were sung on many a hearthstone, in many a public-house, music-hall or homely social gathering. My dad used to sing one called &ldquo; Ben Hobson&rsquo;s Advice to his Sons.&rdquo;</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style="mso-ascii-font-family: Calibri; mso-fareast-font-family: Calibri; mso-hansi-font-family: Calibri; mso-bidi-font-family: 'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style="mso-ascii-font-family: Calibri; mso-fareast-font-family: Calibri; mso-hansi-font-family: Calibri; mso-bidi-font-family: 'Times New Roman';">&ldquo;Ben Hobson sat before the fire, and puffed his bacca smook, <br /> The picture of a good old sire to give and take a joke ; <br /> He&rsquo;d puff away, look wisely round, then wink&rsquo;d at Dick and Dan,<br /> Just like a mortal, wisdom crowned, then to his sons began: <br /> &lsquo;My bonny lads, you've just arrived at th&rsquo;world&rsquo;s uncertain age, <br /> And with my tongue I'll just contrive the lesson of a sage, <br /> Take all th&rsquo;advice that you can get, turn not your head away, <br /> Don&rsquo;t let folks put you in a pet with anything they say,&rdquo; </span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style="mso-ascii-font-family: Calibri; mso-fareast-font-family: Calibri; mso-hansi-font-family: Calibri; mso-bidi-font-family: 'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style="mso-ascii-font-family: Calibri; mso-fareast-font-family: Calibri; mso-hansi-font-family: Calibri; mso-bidi-font-family: 'Times New Roman';">Another old bit was with a very drawling tune, or lack of one.</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style="mso-ascii-font-family: Calibri; mso-fareast-font-family: Calibri; mso-hansi-font-family: Calibri; mso-bidi-font-family: 'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style="mso-ascii-font-family: Calibri; mso-fareast-font-family: Calibri; mso-hansi-font-family: Calibri; mso-bidi-font-family: 'Times New Roman';">&ldquo;When a child I lived at Lincoln with my parents at the farm, <br /> The lessons that my mother taught to me were quite a charm; <br /> She used to take me on her knee when tired of childish play, <br /> And as she pressed me to her breast I&rsquo;ve heard my mother say: <br /> &lsquo;Waste not, want not&rsquo; is a maxim I would teach, <br /> And let your watchword be &lsquo; dispatch,&rsquo; and practise what you preach, <br /> Then do not let your chances like the sunbeams pass you by, <br /> For you'll never miss the water till the well runs dry.&rdquo; </span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style="mso-ascii-font-family: Calibri; mso-fareast-font-family: Calibri; mso-hansi-font-family: Calibri; mso-bidi-font-family: 'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style="mso-ascii-font-family: Calibri; mso-fareast-font-family: Calibri; mso-hansi-font-family: Calibri; mso-bidi-font-family: 'Times New Roman';">One of my own favourite songs as a lad and young man, in addition to the &lsquo;&lsquo;Farmer&rsquo;s Boy &rdquo; (which I have sung in pub and club hundreds of times) was an old North Country ditty, as follows: </span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style="mso-ascii-font-family: Calibri; mso-fareast-font-family: Calibri; mso-hansi-font-family: Calibri; mso-bidi-font-family: 'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style="mso-ascii-font-family: Calibri; mso-fareast-font-family: Calibri; mso-hansi-font-family: Calibri; mso-bidi-font-family: 'Times New Roman';">&ldquo;When ah had wark and brass to spend, <br /> Ah niver wanted for a friend; <br /> Foaks coom a camping ivery neet, <br /> And moved at me when we met i&rsquo; th&rsquo; street. <br /> Mi company wor coorted then <br /> Bi business chaps and gentlemen, <br /> Ah cahnted comrades bi the score <br /> But na ah&rsquo;ve none since ah gat poor,<br style="mso-special-character: line-break;" /> <br style="mso-special-character: line-break;" /> </span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style="mso-ascii-font-family: Calibri; mso-fareast-font-family: Calibri; mso-hansi-font-family: Calibri; mso-bidi-font-family: 'Times New Roman';">Ah&rsquo;d invitations ivery day, <br /> To dine or sup or tak mi tay, <br /> Or goa an&rsquo; have a friendly chat Wi&rsquo; Mr. this, or Mrs. that; <br /> An&rsquo; t&rsquo; squire o&rsquo; consequence to boot, <br /> Wood ask me o&rsquo;er to fish an&rsquo; shoot, <br /> Wi&rsquo; dog an&rsquo; gun o&rsquo;er dale an&rsquo; moor, <br /> But nah that's changed since ah gat poor. </span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style="mso-ascii-font-family: Calibri; mso-fareast-font-family: Calibri; mso-hansi-font-family: Calibri; mso-bidi-font-family: 'Times New Roman';">Then Scotchmen bothered me wi&rsquo; goods, <br /> Wi&rsquo; tongues as smooth as soft soap suds, <br /> Mi patronage, they strove to get it, <br /> Wi&rsquo; yards o&rsquo; cloth an&rsquo; years o&rsquo; credit, <br /> But nah, they&rsquo;ve changed ther tune bi&rsquo;t&rsquo; mass, <br /> They come hawking tay for ready brass, <br /> They carn&rsquo;t see t&rsquo;number on mi door, <br /> They've got so blind since ah gat poor. </span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style="mso-ascii-font-family: Calibri; mso-fareast-font-family: Calibri; mso-hansi-font-family: Calibri; mso-bidi-font-family: 'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style="mso-ascii-font-family: Calibri; mso-fareast-font-family: Calibri; mso-hansi-font-family: Calibri; mso-bidi-font-family: 'Times New Roman';">An&rsquo; what maks me feel varey fow&rsquo; <br /> Mi kinfolks doesna know me now, <br /> Stuck up wi&rsquo; pride to sich a pitch, <br /> Aw&rsquo;v nooa relations but what&rsquo;s rich, <br /> An even my own brother, Jim <br /> He says I&rsquo;m nowt akin to him, <br /> You may live o&rsquo;th air or lig o&rsquo;th floor, <br /> It&rsquo;s good enough for those &lsquo;ats poor.&rdquo; </span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style="mso-ascii-font-family: Calibri; mso-fareast-font-family: Calibri; mso-hansi-font-family: Calibri; mso-bidi-font-family: 'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;</span></p>
