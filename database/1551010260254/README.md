<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0cm 0cm 0.0001pt; font-size: medium; font-family: 'Times New Roman', serif;"><span style="font-size: 11pt;">[Robert Wright, interested in the Orpwood daughter but rejected, was found guilty of breaking the peace and of libel and fined on 7 July 1779. His song included the lines &lsquo;In at the garret window you easily may get / Then into my room you I quickly will let&rsquo;. The lyrics accused Mrs Orpwood of a similar easy access: &lsquo;Since then, my dear mother, your frolicks are known, I hope you will always be ready to own / [&hellip;] Since your case is my case, and my case is thine&rsquo;]</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0cm 0cm 0.0001pt; font-size: medium; font-family: 'Times New Roman', serif;"><span style="font-size: 11pt;">&nbsp;</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0cm 0cm 0.0001pt; font-size: medium; font-family: 'Times New Roman', serif;"><span style="font-size: 11pt;">[Indictment] ROBERT WRIGHT was indicted for that he with a loud voice and in a publick open manner did sing, say, speak, utter, and pronounce divers false, scandalous, infamous, malicious, and obscene songs and and matters of and concerning Elizabeth the wife of Joseph Orpwood and Elizabeth the daughter of the said Joseph reflecting on their character and reputation. 2d Count. That he did cause and procure the said songs, &amp;c. to be sung, said, spoke, uttered and pronounced with the same intent. 3d Count. For publishing the said songs, &amp;c.</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0cm 0cm 0.0001pt; font-size: medium; font-family: 'Times New Roman', serif;"><span style="font-size: 11pt;">&nbsp;</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0cm 0cm 0.0001pt; font-size: medium; font-family: 'Times New Roman', serif;"><span style="font-size: 11pt;">[a copy of the lyrics follows the indictment in the trial record]</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0cm 0cm 0.0001pt; font-size: medium; font-family: 'Times New Roman', serif;"><span style="font-size: 11pt;">&nbsp;</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0cm 0cm 0.0001pt; font-size: medium; font-family: 'Times New Roman', serif;"><span style="font-size: 11pt;">JOSEPH ORPWOOD sworn:&nbsp;</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0cm 0cm 0.0001pt; font-size: medium; font-family: 'Times New Roman', serif;"><span style="font-size: 11pt;">Are you a butcher in&nbsp;Whitechapel-market? - Yes.</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0cm 0cm 0.0001pt; font-size: medium; font-family: 'Times New Roman', serif;"><span style="font-size: 11pt;">&nbsp;</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0cm 0cm 0.0001pt; font-size: medium; font-family: 'Times New Roman', serif;"><span style="font-size: 11pt;">You are married I understand? - Yes.</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0cm 0cm 0.0001pt; font-size: medium; font-family: 'Times New Roman', serif;"><span style="font-size: 11pt;">&nbsp;</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0cm 0cm 0.0001pt; font-size: medium; font-family: 'Times New Roman', serif;"><span style="font-size: 11pt;">And have a daughter? - Yes.</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0cm 0cm 0.0001pt; font-size: medium; font-family: 'Times New Roman', serif;"><span style="font-size: 11pt;">&nbsp;</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0cm 0cm 0.0001pt; font-size: medium; font-family: 'Times New Roman', serif;"><span style="font-size: 11pt;">Do you know the defendant Wright? - Very well, he is a butcher in our neighbourhood.</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0cm 0cm 0.0001pt; font-size: medium; font-family: 'Times New Roman', serif;"><span style="font-size: 11pt;">&nbsp;</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0cm 0cm 0.0001pt; font-size: medium; font-family: 'Times New Roman', serif;"><span style="font-size: 11pt;">He was acquainted with your family at one time? - Very much so.</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0cm 0cm 0.0001pt; font-size: medium; font-family: 'Times New Roman', serif;"><span style="font-size: 11pt;">&nbsp;</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0cm 0cm 0.0001pt; font-size: medium; font-family: 'Times New Roman', serif;"><span style="font-size: 11pt;">At one time he paid his addresses to your daughter? - I do not know that, there was a kind of a detachment two years ago. [&hellip;] In January, and before, there were several of these songs industriously spread about in the market and to some of my customers; they came and told me there were some scandalous songs made, upon my wife and daughter. [&hellip;]&nbsp;&nbsp;I saw John Cooley come out singing them; about six or seven doors before he came to my door. I sent John Cooley to Wright's house, to see if he could get any of the songs of him; I saw Wright deliver the songs into John Cooley 's hand; upon that I went up to John Cooley and said what have you got? what are you singing? some songs, master, says he; I said what&nbsp;songs? he said a copy of verses upon a young lady in Whitechapel-market. How do you sell them? a halfpenny a piece; how many have you got? three; I bought the three songs of him. Wright came up almost close to us; I said where might you get these songs? Cooley said; I bought them of Bob Wright, did not I? Wright made answer, yes you did.</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0cm 0cm 0.0001pt; font-size: medium; font-family: 'Times New Roman', serif;"><span style="font-size: 11pt;">[&hellip;]&nbsp;</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0cm 0cm 0.0001pt; font-size: medium; font-family: 'Times New Roman', serif;"><span style="font-size: 11pt;">&nbsp;</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0cm 0cm 0.0001pt; font-size: medium; font-family: 'Times New Roman', serif;"><span style="font-size: 11pt;">Counsel for the Crown. Need we read the song?</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0cm 0cm 0.0001pt; font-size: medium; font-family: 'Times New Roman', serif;"><span style="font-size: 11pt;">Counsel for the Prisoner. No, I admit it.</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0cm 0cm 0.0001pt; font-size: medium; font-family: 'Times New Roman', serif;"><span style="font-size: 11pt;">[&hellip;]</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0cm 0cm 0.0001pt; font-size: medium; font-family: 'Times New Roman', serif;"><span style="font-size: 11pt;">&nbsp;</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0cm 0cm 0.0001pt; font-size: medium; font-family: 'Times New Roman', serif;"><span style="font-size: 11pt;">THOMAS GAMBLE sworn.</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0cm 0cm 0.0001pt; font-size: medium; font-family: 'Times New Roman', serif;"><span style="font-size: 11pt;">What are you? - A butcher.</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0cm 0cm 0.0001pt; font-size: medium; font-family: 'Times New Roman', serif;"><span style="font-size: 11pt;">&nbsp;</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0cm 0cm 0.0001pt; font-size: medium; font-family: 'Times New Roman', serif;"><span style="font-size: 11pt;">Do you know Wright the defendant? - Yes.</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0cm 0cm 0.0001pt; font-size: medium; font-family: 'Times New Roman', serif;"><span style="font-size: 11pt;">&nbsp;</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0cm 0cm 0.0001pt; font-size: medium; font-family: 'Times New Roman', serif;"><span style="font-size: 11pt;">Do you remember at any time being at a publick-house in Whitechapel? - Yes, at the Bull's-Head in Whitechapel, in January. I bought one of the songs in the Bull's Head ale-house of the draw-boy.</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0cm 0cm 0.0001pt; font-size: medium; font-family: 'Times New Roman', serif;"><span style="font-size: 11pt;">&nbsp;</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0cm 0cm 0.0001pt; font-size: medium; font-family: 'Times New Roman', serif;"><span style="font-size: 11pt;">Did Wright come in soon after the song was bought? - In a few minutes.</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0cm 0cm 0.0001pt; font-size: medium; font-family: 'Times New Roman', serif;"><span style="font-size: 11pt;">What did he say about the song? - I asked him if he had got any of those songs about Betsy Orpwood.</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0cm 0cm 0.0001pt; font-size: medium; font-family: 'Times New Roman', serif;"><span style="font-size: 11pt;">&nbsp;</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0cm 0cm 0.0001pt; font-size: medium; font-family: 'Times New Roman', serif;"><span style="font-size: 11pt;">It was well understood who they related to? - Yes, he made answer, He had, and he would give any body five dozen of them for nothing that would sing them up and down the market.</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0cm 0cm 0.0001pt; font-size: medium; font-family: 'Times New Roman', serif;"><span style="font-size: 11pt;">[&hellip;]</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0cm 0cm 0.0001pt; font-size: medium; font-family: 'Times New Roman', serif;"><span style="font-size: 11pt;">&nbsp;</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0cm 0cm 0.0001pt; font-size: medium; font-family: 'Times New Roman', serif;"><span style="font-size: 11pt;">GUILTY Fined 1 s. and discharged, upon condition of publickly asking pardon of the prosecutor and his family, and paying all the expences&nbsp;<em>[sic]</em>of the prosecution.&nbsp;<span class="apple-converted-space">&nbsp;</span></span></p>
