<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0cm 0cm 0.0001pt; font-size: medium; font-family: 'Times New Roman';"><span style="font-size: 11pt;">[Leonard Ellisdon sang in Church choirs from the age of ten until a stroke in 1957 disabled him].</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0cm 0cm 0.0001pt; font-size: medium; font-family: 'Times New Roman';"><span style="font-size: 11pt;">&nbsp;</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0cm 0cm 0.0001pt; font-size: medium; font-family: 'Times New Roman';"><span style="font-size: 11pt;">Many amusing incidents occurred during these years, far too numerous for me to remember, but a few I do recall might be worth mentioning. I do know that on occasions I have felt really ill with trying not to laugh.</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0cm 0cm 0.0001pt; font-size: medium; font-family: 'Times New Roman';"><span style="font-size: 11pt;">[&hellip;]</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0cm 0cm 0.0001pt; font-size: medium; font-family: 'Times New Roman';"><span style="font-size: 11pt;">&nbsp;</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0cm 0cm 0.0001pt; font-size: medium; font-family: 'Times New Roman';"><span style="font-size: 11pt;">[When I was a boy] we rehearsed an anthem which concluded with three Hallelujahs, each one followed by one bar&rsquo;s tootle on the organ. The boys at rehearsal put words, sotto voice, to these tootles, viz. &ldquo;Skin a donkey&rdquo;. I was still very young and simply sang from memory and had grown accustomed to the boys&rsquo; version, so that when the Sunday evening&nbsp;arrived and the anthem was performed, after the first Hallelujah, I in all innocence I assure you, continued with &ldquo;Skin a donkey&rdquo; to the amazement of the congregation and the amusement of the choir, including some of the men. I don&rsquo;t think anyone was capable of singing the remaining few bars.&nbsp;</span></p>
