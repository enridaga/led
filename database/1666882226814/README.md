<p class="MsoNormal"><span style="mso-ascii-font-family: Calibri; mso-fareast-font-family: Calibri; mso-hansi-font-family: Calibri; mso-bidi-font-family: 'Times New Roman';">[Frame&rsquo;s first circus appearance was] at Hengler&rsquo;s in Wellington Street, at Alfred Powell&rsquo;s benefit on Friday, 1<sup>st</sup> March, 1895.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>I well remember that debut.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>I appeared in my old friend David Abbey Seal&rsquo;s costume.</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style="mso-ascii-font-family: Calibri; mso-fareast-font-family: Calibri; mso-hansi-font-family: Calibri; mso-bidi-font-family: 'Times New Roman';">For a day or two prior to my first appearance, I practised with a horse to learn how to follow it round the ring, a task that is not so easily done as it looks.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>However, I got into the step all right, and was prepared for the event.</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style="mso-ascii-font-family: Calibri; mso-fareast-font-family: Calibri; mso-hansi-font-family: Calibri; mso-bidi-font-family: 'Times New Roman';">I had been &ldquo;starred&rdquo; for that new role, and I looked the jester from tip to toe.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>As I entered the ring to the trumpet calls to jest with the lady on horse-back, who had been going through her wonderful evolutions, I met with a rousing reception.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>I at once began to make love to the &ldquo;Queen of the ring&rdquo; as becomes a fool, singing</span><span style="mso-ascii-font-family: Calibri; mso-fareast-font-family: Calibri; mso-hansi-font-family: Calibri; mso-bidi-font-family: Calibri;">―</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-left: 36.0pt;"><span style="mso-ascii-font-family: Calibri; mso-fareast-font-family: Calibri; mso-hansi-font-family: Calibri; mso-bidi-font-family: 'Times New Roman';">&ldquo;Why should we wait till to-morrow?<br /> You are queen of my heart to-night.&rdquo;</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style="mso-ascii-font-family: Calibri; mso-fareast-font-family: Calibri; mso-hansi-font-family: Calibri; mso-bidi-font-family: 'Times New Roman';">My opening words were:</span><span style="mso-ascii-font-family: Calibri; mso-fareast-font-family: Calibri; mso-hansi-font-family: Calibri; mso-bidi-font-family: Calibri;">―</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-left: 36.0pt;"><span style="mso-ascii-font-family: Calibri; mso-fareast-font-family: Calibri; mso-hansi-font-family: Calibri; mso-bidi-font-family: 'Times New Roman';">&ldquo;Full well I know I am a fool,<br /> That&rsquo;s just why I am here:<br /> It takes a fool the whole world over,<br /> To lend wise people cheer.<br /> So don&rsquo;t be hasty, if you please,<br /> But keep your temper cool,<br /> And you shall hear a little sense<br /> From me, though just a fool.&rdquo;</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style="mso-ascii-font-family: Calibri; mso-fareast-font-family: Calibri; mso-hansi-font-family: Calibri; mso-bidi-font-family: 'Times New Roman';">&nbsp;</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style="mso-ascii-font-family: Calibri; mso-fareast-font-family: Calibri; mso-hansi-font-family: Calibri; mso-bidi-font-family: 'Times New Roman';">It was a big night for me, and I had to sing several songs before leaving the ring, among them being my popular impersonation of &ldquo;The Sweep.&rdquo;<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>The chorus ran:</span><span style="mso-ascii-font-family: Calibri; mso-fareast-font-family: Calibri; mso-hansi-font-family: Calibri; mso-bidi-font-family: Calibri;">―</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-left: 36.0pt;"><span style="mso-ascii-font-family: Calibri; mso-fareast-font-family: Calibri; mso-hansi-font-family: Calibri; mso-bidi-font-family: 'Times New Roman';">&ldquo;Sweep! Sweep! you&rsquo;re sure to hear my cry,<br /> Early in the mornin&rsquo; when I&rsquo;m passin&rsquo; by;<br /> The boys a&rsquo; ken me an&rsquo; cry Macintyre,<br /> Rin for a bowl o&rsquo; watter, there&rsquo;s a lum on fire.&rdquo;</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style="mso-ascii-font-family: Calibri; mso-fareast-font-family: Calibri; mso-hansi-font-family: Calibri; mso-bidi-font-family: 'Times New Roman';">It was while I was at Hengler&rsquo;s that I introduced my popular song, &ldquo;It&rsquo;s a braw, bricht, munelicht nicht the nicht, hooch aye,&rdquo; and it proved an instantaneous success.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>That night</span><span style="mso-ascii-font-family: Calibri; mso-fareast-font-family: Calibri; mso-hansi-font-family: Calibri; mso-bidi-font-family: Calibri;">―</span><span style="mso-ascii-font-family: Calibri; mso-fareast-font-family: Calibri; mso-hansi-font-family: Calibri; mso-bidi-font-family: 'Times New Roman';">although a turn on the sawdust was somewhat fresh to me</span><span style="mso-ascii-font-family: Calibri; mso-fareast-font-family: Calibri; mso-hansi-font-family: Calibri; mso-bidi-font-family: Calibri;">―</span><span style="mso-ascii-font-family: Calibri; mso-fareast-font-family: Calibri; mso-hansi-font-family: Calibri; mso-bidi-font-family: 'Times New Roman';">I am pleased to record that my show was appreciated by the vast audience.</span></p>
