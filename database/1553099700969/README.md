### comment


Henry Croswell (1840–93) kept a record of his visits to churches in London over a period of more than twelve years (1872–85).  He made methodical notes about the number of clergy, the churchmanship, the congregation, the sermon and the church architecture, as well as commenting on the music that he heard (the organ, the hymns and the choir).  The above listening experience has been extracted from one of these records.
‘Bickers’ refers to ‘The Hymnal Companion to the Book of Common Prayer’ (1870, rev. 1877; and in a revised edition with tunes, 1890) compiled by Edward Henry Bickersteth (1825–1906).

<p>Got there 6.50.</p>
<p class="MsoNormal">[&hellip;]</p>
<p class="MsoNormal">O[rgan]. &ndash;&nbsp;Ordinary, old, middling played.</p>
<p class="MsoNormal">H[ymns]. &ndash;&nbsp;Bickers, old edition.&nbsp; "My God, my Father &hellip;" and "O Paradise, O Paradise [&hellip;"]</p>
<p class="MsoNormal">C[hoir]. &ndash;&nbsp;Badly behaved, very mixed in West Gallery.&nbsp; Charity girls round Altar rails.&nbsp; They sing Psalms alternately [? by alternate verses], nice effect.</p>
<p class="MsoNormal">[The congregation numbered] 300 &ndash;&nbsp;Doubtless many more in the <span style="text-decoration: underline;">morning</span>. [&hellip;] Very ordinary lot from S. John's Wood.&nbsp;</p>
