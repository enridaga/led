### comment


Henry Croswell (1840–93) kept a record of his visits to churches in London over a period of more than twelve years (1872–85).  He made methodical notes about the number of clergy, the churchmanship, the congregation, the sermon and the church architecture, as well as commenting on the music that he heard (the organ, the hymns and the choir).  The above listening experience has been extracted from one of these records.  Croswell had previously visited St Paul’s Cathedral on 16 January 1876: see LE no. 1547033299124.

<p>We left before the Celebration [of communion].&nbsp; So did most people.</p>
<p class="MsoNormal">[&hellip;]</p>
<p class="MsoNormal">O[rgan]. &ndash;&nbsp;Grand and old and finely played.</p>
<p class="MsoNormal">C[hoir]. &ndash;&nbsp;Twenty boys and twenty men with magnificent voices.</p>
<p class="MsoNormal">H[ymns]. &ndash;&nbsp;No hymn or Anthem but a grand choral service.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>
<p class="MsoNormal">[The congregation numbered] Say 2000 present.&nbsp; &nbsp;There were many sight-seers but not in the choir but in the nave.</p>
<p class="MsoNormal">[&hellip;]</p>
<p class="MsoNormal">M[iscellaneous]. &ndash;&nbsp;It is a great success under the present Dean and Chapter, a <span style="text-decoration: underline;">great contrast to former times</span>.&nbsp;</p>
