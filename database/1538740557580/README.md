<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0cm 0cm 0.0001pt; font-size: medium; font-family: 'Times New Roman';"><span style="font-size: 11pt;">My first hymn, learned at St. Silas&rsquo;s C.E. school, was &ldquo;Jesus bids us shine&rdquo;, each verse ending &ldquo;You in your small corner, and I in mine.&rdquo; I imagined Jesus as being like one of my own elder brothers, but even kinder, for He wouldn&rsquo;t ask me to leave my play to do things for Him, as the boys did. He lived in my singing of the hymn as the fifth person in a game I&rsquo;d watched the big (8-year!) girls playing, &ldquo;Pussy Four Corners.&rdquo; Four girls occupied the four corners, and dodged from one to the other while the unfortunate fifth, in the middle, tried to occupy a corner during the moment&rsquo;s vacating of it. Yes, I saw it all: Jesus, in his white sheet, wouldn&rsquo;t mind being the one left out all the time: He was so kind. So I understood why we sang about &ldquo;You in your small corner and I-in-mine&rdquo; &ndash; each clutching a little Christmas-tree candle.&nbsp;</span></p>
