<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0cm 0cm 0.0001pt; font-size: medium; font-family: 'Times New Roman';"><span style="font-size: 11pt;">To return to the characters and well known personalities of the old Hunslet would not be right without mentioning a well known Hunslet worthy of that time, namely John Spurr.&nbsp;</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0cm 0cm 0.0001pt; font-size: medium; font-family: 'Times New Roman';"><span style="font-size: 11pt;">&nbsp;</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0cm 0cm 0.0001pt; font-size: medium; font-family: 'Times New Roman';"><span style="font-size: 11pt;">He lived and carried on his proffession [<em>sic</em>] as a &ldquo;Vet&rdquo; [&hellip;] [in] a large open area paved with stone setts [.]</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0cm 0cm 0.0001pt; font-size: medium; font-family: 'Times New Roman';"><span style="font-size: 11pt;">[&hellip;]</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0cm 0cm 0.0001pt; font-size: medium; font-family: 'Times New Roman';"><span style="font-size: 11pt;">&nbsp;</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0cm 0cm 0.0001pt; font-size: medium; font-family: 'Times New Roman';"><span style="font-size: 11pt;">It was here that John Spurr used to hold his &ldquo;Surgery&rdquo; in any spare time he might have. Apart from his work as a &ldquo;Oss Doctor&rdquo;, he was a Council member, and he would give advice on any matters arising there. He was also the organiser of &ldquo;Wiffem Waffem&rdquo; Band. This was a group of local chaps who dressed up in a weird assortment of comic costumes, and they paraded the local streets playing an assortment of &ldquo;Instruments&rdquo; as weird as the outfits they wore.&nbsp;</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0cm 0cm 0.0001pt; font-size: medium; font-family: 'Times New Roman';"><span style="font-size: 11pt;">&nbsp;</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0cm 0cm 0.0001pt; font-size: medium; font-family: 'Times New Roman';"><span style="font-size: 11pt;">These varied from mouth organs, tin whistles and &ldquo;Kazoos&rdquo; to old gramophone trumpets, and sheet metal tubes, to dustbin lids for drums and pan lids for cymbals. Spurr himself played a concertina. They collected from passers by and anyone else who came out to watch them, and all the proceeds went towards treats for poor children.&nbsp;</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0cm 0cm 0.0001pt; font-size: medium; font-family: 'Times New Roman';"><span style="font-size: 11pt;">&nbsp;</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0cm 0cm 0.0001pt; font-size: medium; font-family: 'Times New Roman';"><span style="font-size: 11pt;">Needless to say their repertoire was more discordant than melodious, but the end justified the means used.&nbsp;</span></p>
