<p>Spent the afternoon listening to Eugene practice his Debussy group. Those French waters washed me back to Lake Michigan's adolescent waves, waves which were <em>La Mer</em> in 1937, and swept me then to Vuillardian parlors where, musically submerged in <em>vin rouge</em> and fumes of Camembert, the composer composed <em>Khamma</em>. But it can be summoned clear as a snapshot of the Parc Monceau, and the sound of a series of secondary sevenths is enough for the snapshot to smell of almond leaves.</p>
